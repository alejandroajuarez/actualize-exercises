How to Make Nuka-Cola!

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz cola soda (e.g., Coca-Cola or Pepsi)
  • 3 oz lemon-lime soda (e.g., Sprite or 7-Up)
  • 1 oz cream soda
  • 1/2 tsp grenadine syrup (for a hint of sweetness and red tint)
  • Ice cubes (optional)
  • Blue food coloring (optional, for a glowing Nuka-Cola Quantum effect)

Instructions:

  1. Combine the base sodas: In a tall glass, mix the cola, lemon-lime soda, and cream soda.
  2. Add grenadine: Stir in the grenadine syrup gently to maintain carbonation.
  3. Optional glow: If you want to recreate the look of Nuka-Cola Quantum, add 1-2 drops of blue food coloring and mix.
  4. Serve: Add ice cubes to a glass or serve chilled. For an authentic touch, use a glass bottle with a custom Nuka-Cola label.
  5. Enjoy: Sip your drink while imagining yourself in the Wasteland!

How to Make Deathclaw Steak!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large ribeye steak (about 12 oz)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme (chopped)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the steak: Pat the steak dry with paper towels. Rub olive oil over both sides and season generously with salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, and cayenne (optional).
  2. Sear the steak: Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. When hot, add the steak and sear for 3-4 minutes per side until a crust forms.
  3. Add aromatics: Lower the heat slightly and add butter, minced garlic, rosemary, and thyme. Spoon the melted butter and herbs over the steak for another 2 minutes.
  4. Check doneness: Use a meat thermometer (125Â°F for medium-rare). Adjust cooking time to your preference.
  5. Rest and serve: Let the steak rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Serve with a side of mashed potatoes or grilled veggies.

How to Make Mirelurk Cakes!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lump crab meat
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (plus extra for coating)
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter (for frying)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Mix ingredients: In a bowl, combine crab meat, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, Old Bay seasoning, smoked paprika, egg, breadcrumbs, and parsley. Gently mix until just combined.
  2. Form cakes: Shape the mixture into 6-8 patties. Coat each patty lightly in breadcrumbs for extra crunch.
  3. Chill: Refrigerate the cakes for 20-30 minutes to help them hold together during cooking.
  4. Cook: Heat butter in a skillet over medium heat. Fry the crab cakes for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve: Serve hot with lemon wedges and a side of tartar sauce.
